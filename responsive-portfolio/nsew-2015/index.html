Deliver Great Customer Service

I was awful at selling my first day. I literally went in a circle of a customer asking me how the pants looked and me asking her if they fit her well. I later learned how clothes properly fit and could serve the customers better.

A good listener is a good sales person

The more you listen to your customer the better you can sell to them. For me my designs are easier to sell to them if I listened to their needs.

Do Whatever the job requires

I cleaned toilets, took out trash, and cleaned tracked poop off the floor

People will try to steal from you

Every once in awhile a person would come in and try to steal from the store. The regulation was we could try to stop them in the store by being extra attentative, but could not do anything once they left the store. We would follow the perp and ask if she needed help when she was shopping and when she was trying clothes on. We had to remember every article or clothing or jewelry she had, so we could ask her how it looked. If she knew we knew what she had, she was less likely to steal. After she left, we would watch where she went next and notify the next store.

In business, you do everything you can to not get screwed. You follow up by email and phone call and send invoices. Worst case, lawyer get involved and contracts get better. At some point you will get screwed over by a client, but it is due diligance to warn others from working with the client.

How to get along with people

When the customer came in, your focus was them even if you had four other customers in other fitting rooms. They ask you for help. You help. If they want to tell you about their son or job, you listen. It helps gain the trust of the customer.

Another good resource is How to Win Friends and Influence People

Network

I networked with customers all the time. I would do everyting I could to help the customer, and then find out what they did for a living.

Your coworkers don't have to be your best friends

I enjoyed working with the men and women at Loft, but my goal wasn't to make friends. If you do form long lasting relationships, then that is a perk, but not a requirement.

Talk to anyone & everyone

I would talk to the tallest or the meanest looking woman, and try to help her find clothes or accessories to wear.

This skill proved useful when I had to talk to Colonels, Major Generals, or CEOs when I was a contractor for the U.S. Army. I will talk to anyone.

After Graduation Reflections

I had a guaranteed paycheck when I left Towson

Even though the job I had when I graduated was the Loft and an unpaid internship, I knew I was still getting a paycheck.

Try out as many industries as possible while you are a student

Once you are working, it is harder to get out of the industry you first chose out of school. You can still leave, but after awhile, you need find a focus.
